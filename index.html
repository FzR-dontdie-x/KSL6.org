About Our Lodge

King Solomon Lodge #6 is one of Tennessee's most historic Masonic lodges, located in Gallatin, Tennessee. We are a community of men committed to personal growth, charitable works, and the betterment of society through the timeless principles of Brotherly Love, Relief, and Truth.

Our Rich History

A Founding Lodge of Tennessee

Originally chartered in North Carolina as King Solomon Lodge #52, our lodge received its Tennessee charter in 1808. As a pioneer lodge, King Solomon Lodge #6 and its members helped form the Grand Lodge of Tennessee on December 27, 1813.

Reclaiming Our Number

In 2014, during Grand Lodge week, a historic moment occurred. After being known as Lodge #94 for 174 years, our lodge successfully petitioned to reclaim our original number #6, which was granted to us as a founding member of the Grand Lodge of Tennessee. Special thanks to Brother Ethan Slarke, Brother Robert Wade Lankford and all the brethren who worked tirelessly to restore this important piece of our heritage.

1808

King Solomon Lodge receives its charter in Tennessee, establishing itself as one of the pioneer lodges in the region.

1813

Our members help establish the Grand Lodge of Tennessee on December 27, marking a pivotal moment in Tennessee Masonic history.

2014

After 174 years as Lodge #94, we successfully reclaim our founding number, once again becoming King Solomon Lodge #6.

Our Three Great Pillars

Brotherly Love

We cultivate bonds of friendship and mutual support among our members, treating each brother with respect, kindness, and understanding.

Relief

We provide assistance to those in need, supporting our brothers, their families, and our community through charitable works and mutual aid.

Truth

We pursue knowledge and wisdom, striving to live with integrity and honor while seeking light in all its forms.

Becoming a Mason

Freemasonry is open to men of good character who believe in a Supreme Being and seek to improve themselves while serving their community. The journey begins with a petition and leads to lifelong brotherhood.

Requirements

Be a man of at least 18 years of age, believe in a Supreme Being, be of good moral character, and come of your own free will.

The Process

Submit a petition, meet with members, receive approval from the lodge, and participate in three meaningful degrees of initiation.

What to Expect

Lifelong friendships, personal growth, opportunities to serve, and connection to a centuries-old tradition of excellence.
